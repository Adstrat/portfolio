Meet App

A serverless, Progressive Web Application allowing users to search for events on Google Calender.

Meet App was built with REACT. Users can search for events in different cities and choose the number of events to be displayed.

At the start of this project I set up AWS Lambda and added the authentication code. This was a steep learning curve, and I got stuck for a while with a bug, but after some perserverence I managed to resolve it.

During the build, unit, integration and end-to-end tests were implemented. Initially it seemed like a strange concept for me to write the tests before the actual code, but I appreciate how this can save time in the long-run.

There were times when I would hit a problem, trying to get something to display a certain way, but there's nothing more satisfying than resolving it. And sometimes discovering a neat trick or a way to do things that was better than my original idea. There's always lots of cool stuff to learn!

All about me

Originally from England, I have bachelors degree in Politics, Philosopy and Economics, a diploma in hypnotherapy, and have spent a lot of time exploring meditation and yoga. I spent many years doing social work, supporting people with autism and learning disabilities.

A few years ago I moved to Berlin with my family and decided to pursue a career in web development, out of a desire to be involved in helping to steer technology in a beneficial direction. And in July 2021 I completed a Full Stack Web-Development Bootcamp at CareerFoundry.

I've always enjoyed creating, in a structured kind of way. Problem solving, making things fit, or work well, is somehow rewarding. I like that sense of completion, when things come together, to flow beautifully.

Some years ago I studied carpentry, just for fun, and I love making functional, built-in furniture. I get that same feeling of being in the groove from programming, especially front-end, when playing around with the look of a website, until it feels just right.

I think in this modern, technological world, there's a chance to make an impact. The internet is a mega powerful tool, which can be harnessed for great things. And it's my hope that I can contribute my skills towards improving the world in some way.

I love front-end development, have some knowledge of back-end and also enjoy dipping my toes into designing the user-interface. In the future I'd love to deepen my knowledge into the rules and tools of creating an easeful user experince.

Aside from programming, I also enjoy juggling, painting, writing and being in nature.
